PET Guide Procedures

In radiology, "PET" typically refers to "Positron Emission Tomography," which is a nuclear medicine imaging technique used to observe metabolic processes in the body. It's often combined with CT (Computed Tomography) to provide both functional and anatomical information in a single scan (PET/CT). PET scans detect radiation emitted by a tracer substance (radioactive glucose or other molecules) injected into the body. This helps visualize metabolic activity and cell function. Radiologists analyze PET/CT images to identify abnormalities, assess treatment response, and guide further management. PET/CT combines PET's functional information with CT's structural detail.
