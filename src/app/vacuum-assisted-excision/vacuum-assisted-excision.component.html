Vacuum Assisted Excision Procedures

Vacuum-Assisted Excision (VAE) is a minimally invasive procedure similar to Vacuum-Assisted Biopsy (VAB) but is used for the removal of benign or suspicious breast lesions rather than just sampling them. VAE is performed under the guidance of imaging modalities such as mammography, ultrasound, or MRI to precisely locate and target the lesion. The procedure is conducted under local anesthesia to ensure patient comfort. A specialized needle or probe equipped with a vacuum mechanism is inserted into the breast tissue. The vacuum helps to draw the targeted lesion into the device. The device cuts and removes the lesion in small pieces, which are then collected in a chamber. This process is repeated until the entire lesion is excised.
