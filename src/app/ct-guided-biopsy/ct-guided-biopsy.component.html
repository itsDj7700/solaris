CT Guided Biopsies Procedures

A CT-guided biopsy is a medical procedure in which a computed tomography (CT) scanner is used to guide the removal of a small sample of tissue from a specific area in the body for examination. This technique is particularly useful for obtaining tissue samples from deep-seated or hard-to-reach areas where more traditional biopsy methods may be difficult or impossible.

The patient may need to fast for several hours before the procedure. Blood tests may be conducted to ensure normal clotting. The patient will be asked to lie on a CT scanner table in a position that provides the best access to the biopsy site.
